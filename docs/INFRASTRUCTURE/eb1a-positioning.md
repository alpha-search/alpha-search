# EB1A Extraordinary Ability Positioning Strategy

> **Purpose:** Position Alpha Search and related activities as evidence of extraordinary ability for EB1A (Extraordinary Ability) green card petition
> **Target Audience:** Immigration attorney preparing I-140 petition
> **EB1A Criteria:** This document maps project activities to USCIS EB1A criteria

---

## Overview

The EB1A "Extraordinary Ability" green card category requires demonstrating sustained national or international acclaim in your field. For a technologist/quantitative researcher, open-source project leadership is powerful evidence.

Alpha Search is positioned as **Evidence of Extraordinary Ability in Quantitative Finance Technology** -- specifically at the intersection of open-source software engineering and quantitative financial research.

### Letter Content Guidelines

Each letter should address:

1. **Relationship:** How the writer knows you and for how long
2. **Your achievements:** Specific accomplishments they can verify
3. **Industry standing:** Their own credentials (why their opinion matters)
4. **Impact assessment:** Specific impact of your work
5. **Comparative standing:** How you compare to others in the field

**Sample ask email:**

```
Subject: Recommendation letter request -- EB1A petition

Dear [Name],

I hope this message finds you well. I'm reaching out because I'm
preparing an EB1A (Extraordinary Ability) green card petition, and I
would be honored if you could provide a recommendation letter.

I've been building Alpha Search, an open-source quantitative research
framework that has grown to [X stars] on GitHub and [Y downloads] on
PyPI. Your perspective as [their role] would carry significant weight
with USCIS.

The letter would focus on:
- [Specific area based on their expertise]
- [Specific achievement they can speak to]
- [Impact metrics they can validate]

I've attached a draft outline and my CV for your reference. There's no
obligation, and I completely understand if your schedule doesn't permit.

Thank you for considering this request.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```
